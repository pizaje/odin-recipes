Description

An Arepa is a patty made of corndough, then cut in half after cooking and stuffed with a very large variety of ingredients. It's a food native to Latin America, being very popular due to its inexpensiveness, low kitchen utility requirements, and high ingredient versatility, making it a favorite of many countries such as Colombia and Venezuela.

Ingredients

    For the shell

    • Corn Dough
    • Water
    • Salt
    • Oil

    For the stuffing

    Your choice of

    • Cheese
    • Sour Cream
    • Butter
    • Olive Oil
    • Guacamole or Avocado
    • Beans
    • Peppers
    • Eggs
    • Beef
    • Chicken
    • Ham
    • Bacon
    • ..and pretty much anything else!

Recipe

  1. Pour dough into a large bowl
  2. Pour water into the dough until its level is one finger above the dough
  3. Add salt and a small amount of oil to the mixture
  4. Use your hands to thoroughly mash the ingredients together
  5. Take out a handful of dough mix and pad it down to a disc with the thickness of your finger and the size of your palm
  6. Place the dough in your hands in an oiled flat pan on a stove at medium heat. Traditionally, around 3-4 arepas are made on one pan
  7. Flip after 10-20 minutes
  8. After the shell is golden, take the arepas out and cut them in half, use gloves to not get burned
  9. Add any ingredients you want
